Why invest in derivatives?

Derivatives are a form of financial contract

Between two or more parties based upon the assets. The most common form of derivatives are futures, forwards, options, swaps.

They can be used to hedge or insure against risk on an asset

It can also be used for speculation in betting on the future price of an assets

Introduction to Futures and Forwards

Futures are available to be traded on an exchange and, therefore, are standardized contracts. Futures contracts have clearing houses that guarantees the transaction which reduce the probability of defaulting.

Forwards are private agreements between two parties and it is only available in the OTC (Over-the counter). It is a private agreement between both parties have a higher risk of one party defaulting on the side of the agreement.

Introduction to Swaps and Options

A contract that binds between the party that gives the buyer the right to buy (call) or sell (put) the underlying securities at an agreed upon price (strike price)

Exchange of one financial instrument for another between the parties
Examples: Interest rate swaps, Currency swaps, Credit-default swaps
